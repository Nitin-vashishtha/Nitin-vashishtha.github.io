About

Solar Physics at the Frontier

I am a solar physicist fascinated by the most energetic phenomena in our solar system. My research centers on understanding how the Sun's magnetic field erupts, drives coronal mass ejections (CMEs), and shapes the heliospheric environment that envelopes Earth.

Working at the intersection of theory and observation, I combine advanced magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) simulations — primarily with the MPI-AMRVAC framework — with multi-wavelength data from missions including Parker Solar Probe, WIND, and SDO/AIA. Forward modeling techniques let me generate synthetic observables for direct comparison with spacecraft data.

Beyond CME physics, I explore the application of machine learning to heliophysics — from automated flare detection to probabilistic space weather forecasting and physics-informed neural networks for plasma dynamics.

Research Areas

What I Work On

🌞

CME Initiation & Propagation

Numerical modeling of coronal mass ejection launch mechanisms — flux rope emergence, the breakout model, torus instability — and propagation through the inner heliosphere using adaptive mesh refinement.

🔬

Forward Modeling

Synthesizing white-light coronagraph and EUV images from 3D MHD simulation outputs to bridge the gap between theoretical models and direct spacecraft observations.

⚡

Magnetic Reconnection

Studying current sheet formation, tearing-mode instabilities, and plasmoid dynamics during solar eruptions, including the role of the heliospheric current sheet in CME evolution.

🌊

Wave Dynamics & Plumes

Investigating velocity dispersion and oscillatory behavior in solar polar plumes, Alfvén wave propagation, and compressive wave modes in coronal and chromospheric structures.

🛰

Multi-Spacecraft Analysis

Combining in-situ measurements from Parker Solar Probe, WIND, ACE, and STEREO with remote-sensing data to track heliospheric structures and CME geo-effectiveness.

🤖

AI / ML in Heliophysics

Applying machine learning to solar flare prediction, automated CME detection in coronagraph imagery, and physics-informed neural networks for magnetohydrodynamic problems.
